Chapter I The Story

In the year 1300, the poet Dante Alighieri finds himself lost in a dark forest. Guided by the soul of Virgil, he embarks on a journey through the Nine Circles of Hell — an eternal realm of torment populated by demons, monsters, and damned souls.

In Inferno Quest, you are Dante. You explore a vast open-world RPG inspired by the Divine Comedy. Every circle is a unique biome with its own enemies, quests, secrets, and lore to uncover. The world breathes — roam freely, discover hidden passages, and forge your own path through the darkness.

Your goal: reach the frozen core of Hell, defeat Lucifer, and find your way back to the light.

Chapter II The Open World

Hell is not a straight road. Inferno Quest gives you complete freedom to explore each circle at your own pace. Venture off the main path to discover hidden shrines, secret dungeons, rare creatures, and forgotten relics.

Free Exploration
Roam every circle without restrictions. No invisible walls, no forced paths.
Side Quests
Help — or betray — the damned souls you meet. Every choice has consequences.
Hidden Areas
Crack the walls of Hell. Secret passages hide the rarest loot and creatures.
Dynamic World
Enemies patrol, respond to your actions, and grow stronger as you descend.

Use the Hell Map (Tab) to track discovered areas, mark points of interest, and plan your descent. Fog of war hides unexplored regions — nothing is revealed until you walk through it yourself.

Chapter IV Combat System

Combat in Inferno Quest is real-time with tactical depth. You fight directly — dodge, block, and strike in fluid motion. When you encounter a damned soul or a creature of Hell, battle begins immediately in the open world. No loading screens, no separate arenas.

Every enemy has an attack pattern you can learn. Watch for telegraphed moves, find the opening, and punish. Timing your dodge at the last moment triggers a Perfect Evade — slowing time briefly and opening a window for a powerful counter.

Light Attack
Fast strikes with low stamina cost. Good for building combos.
Heavy Attack
Slow but devastating. Breaks enemy guards and staggers larger foes.
Special Ability
Dante's class powers — arcane spells, shadow strikes, sacred light, or war cries.
Capture
Weaken a soul below 25% HP, then press F to attempt capture with an Infernal Seal.

Every creature has elemental resistances tied to its circle of origin. Fire creatures resist flames. Ice dwellers shrug off cold. Study your enemy's weaknesses before diving in — in Hell, arrogance is a death sentence.

Chapter V Character Statistics

Dante has six core statistics. They grow through experience, relics found in the world, and choices made during quests. Invest wisely — the deep circles demand specialization.

Soul (HP)
Your life force. Reach zero and Dante is cast back to the last Waypoint.
Faith
Fuels divine abilities and prayers. Depleted by spells, restored at shrines.
Strength
Physical damage and the ability to break enemy defenses.
Mind
Power of poetic incantations and Commedia-based spells.
Agility
Movement speed, dodge distance, and attack speed. Critical for Assassins.
Fortune
Capture rate, rare item drops, and critical hit probability.
Chapter VI The Nine Circles

Hell is divided into nine concentric circles, each dedicated to a category of sin. The deeper you go, the more dangerous the world becomes — and the more powerful the creatures, the rarer the rewards.

IX Cocytus — Lucifer Final Boss
Chapter VII Capturing the Damned

Captured souls become your allies in combat. To catch one, weaken it below 25% HP in the open world, then press F to use an Infernal Seal.

Capture rate depends on your Fortune stat and the creature's rarity. Bosses cannot be captured, but defeating them unlocks powerful Pact Relics that grant you a fraction of their power.

You can carry up to 6 damned allies in your active party. The rest are stored in your Personal Limbo, accessible from any Waypoint.

Chapter VIII Waypoints & Saving

Scattered across Hell are Waypoints — ancient stone pillars marked with Dante's crest. Interact with one to:

Save Progress
Your game is saved. If you die, you return here with half your carried gold.
Fast Travel
Teleport to any previously discovered Waypoint across all Nine Circles.
Manage Party
Swap allies from your Personal Limbo, heal, and equip relics.
Restore Faith
Your Faith meter is fully restored every time you activate a Waypoint.
